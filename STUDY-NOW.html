Q1 · ICSE 2024 · Intra-state[4 marks]
A shopkeeper marks an article at ₹15,000 and gives a discount of 20%. GST is charged at 18% (intra-state). Find the CGST, SGST, and the amount paid by the customer.
1
SP = 15,000 × 80/100 = ₹12,000
2
CGST Rate = SGST Rate = 18/2 = 9% each
3
CGST = 9/100 × 12,000 = ₹1,080; SGST = ₹1,080
4
Bill = 12,000 + 1,080 + 1,080 = ₹14,160
✓ CGST = ₹1,080 | SGST = ₹1,080 | Bill = ₹14,160
Q2 · ICSE 2023 · Reverse[3 marks]
After a 10% discount and 12% GST, a customer pays ₹28,224. Find the marked price.
1
Let MP = x. SP = x × 90/100
2
Bill = SP × 112/100 → x × 90/100 × 112/100 = 28,224
3
x × 10080/10000 = 28,224 → x = 28,224 × 10000/10080 = ₹28,000
✓ Marked Price = ₹28,000

Strategy: Prove = work ONE side only. Evaluate = replace complementary angles first (cos70°=sin20°). Stuck = convert to sinA/cosA.

Q · 2024 · Prove (a³+b³ trick)[4 marks]
Prove: (sin³θ + cos³θ)/(sinθ + cosθ) + sinθcosθ = 1
1
Use: a³+b³ = (a+b)(a²−ab+b²)
2
Numerator = (sinθ+cosθ)(sin²θ−sinθcosθ+cos²θ)
3
(sinθ+cosθ) cancels → LHS = (1−sinθcosθ) + sinθcosθ = 1 = RHS ✓
✓ Proved using a³+b³ factorisation
Q · 2014 · Evaluate (complementary)[3 marks]
Evaluate: sin²34° + sin²56° + 2tan18°·tan72° − cot²30°

sin56° = cos34° → sin²34° + cos²34° = 1
2
tan72° = cot18° → 2·tan18°·cot18° = 2×1 = 2
3

4
Total = 1 + 2 − 3 = 0

Q · 2018 · Prove (bracket expansion)[4 marks]
Prove: (1 + cotθ − cosecθ)(1 + tanθ + secθ) = 2
1
= [(sinθ+cosθ−1)/sinθ] × [(cosθ+sinθ+1)/cosθ]
2
= (sinθ+cosθ−1)(sinθ+cosθ+1) / (sinθcosθ)
3
= [(sinθ+cosθ)²−1] / sinθcosθ = [1+2sinθcosθ−1] / sinθcosθ
4
= 2sinθcosθ / sinθcosθ = 2 = RHS ✓
✓ Proved

Strategy: Draw diagram → label θ, h, d → write tan θ = h/d → solve. For 2 triangles: write both equations, eliminate one unknown.

Q · Pole & Tower (classic type)[4 marks]
A vertical pole 6 m tall and a vertical tower stand on the same ground. From the top of the pole, the angle of elevation of the top of the tower is 60° and angle of depression of the foot is 30°. Find the height of the tower and distance between them.
1
Depression of foot: tan30° = 6/d → d = 6√3 m
2
Elevation of top: tan60° = (H−6)/d → √3 = (H−6)/(6√3) → H−6 = 18
3
H = 18 + 6 = 24 m | distance = 6√3 ≈ 10.39 m
✓ Tower height = 24 m | Distance = 6√3 m
Q · Two Towers (classic type)[4 marks]
Two vertical towers stand 120 m apart. Angle of elevation of top of Tower 1 from foot of Tower 2 is 30°. Angle of elevation of top of Tower 2 from foot of Tower 1 is 60°. Find heights of both towers.
1
Tower 1: tan30° = h₁/120 → h₁ = 120 × 1/√3 = 40√3 m ≈ 69.3 m
2
Tower 2: tan60° = h₂/120 → h₂ = 120√3 ≈ 207.8 m
✓ h₁ = 40√3 m | h₂ = 120√3 m
