7. Areas of Similar Triangles

The surprising result​

You already know: if two triangles are similar with scale factor kkk, every side is multiplied by kkk.

Here is the result that surprises almost every student:

Area Ratio Theorem

If △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR with scale factor kkk, then:

Area of △PQRArea of △ABC=k2\dfrac{\text{Area of } \triangle PQR}{\text{Area of } \triangle ABC} = k^2Area of △ABCArea of △PQR​=k2

The area ratio is the square of the linear scale factor.

Side ratio = kkk. Area ratio = k2k^2k2. NOT kkk.

Why k² and not k? — The visual proof​

The key insight: area is two-dimensional. When you scale both length AND width by kkk, the area scales by k×k=k2k \times k = k^2k×k=k2.

A triangle is just like the square — it has two dimensions. Double every side → area becomes 2×2=42 \times 2 = 42×2=4 times larger.

See it in similar triangles​

The formula — different forms​

Area1Area2=(corresponding side1corresponding side2)2=k2\dfrac{\text{Area}_1}{\text{Area}_2} = \left(\dfrac{\text{corresponding side}_1}{\text{corresponding side}_2}\right)^2 = k^2Area2​Area1​​=(corresponding side2​corresponding side1​​)2=k2

This can be rewritten with any corresponding measurement:

Area(△ABC)Area(△PQR)=(ABPQ)2=(BCQR)2=(CARP)2\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)}{\text{Area}(\triangle PQR)} = \left(\dfrac{AB}{PQ}\right)^2 = \left(\dfrac{BC}{QR}\right)^2 = \left(\dfrac{CA}{RP}\right)^2Area(△PQR)Area(△ABC)​=(PQAB​)2=(QRBC​)2=(RPCA​)2

Q: △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR with AB=6AB = 6AB=6 and PQ=9PQ = 9PQ=9. Find the ratio of their areas.

Step 1 — Find k: k=PQAB=96=32k = \dfrac{PQ}{AB} = \dfrac{9}{6} = \dfrac{3}{2}k=ABPQ​=69​=23​

Step 2 — Area ratio: Area(△PQR)Area(△ABC)=k2=(32)2=94\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle PQR)}{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)} = k^2 = \left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{9}{4}Area(△ABC)Area(△PQR)​=k2=(23​)2=49​

Answer: Area of △PQR\triangle PQR△PQR : Area of △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC = 9:49 : 49:4

Q: △ABC∼△DEF\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF△ABC∼△DEF. AB=4AB = 4AB=4, DE=6DE = 6DE=6, Area of △ABC=32\triangle ABC = 32△ABC=32 cm². Find area of △DEF\triangle DEF△DEF.

Step 1: k=DEAB=64=32k = \dfrac{DE}{AB} = \dfrac{6}{4} = \dfrac{3}{2}k=ABDE​=46​=23​

Step 2: Area(△DEF)Area(△ABC)=k2=94\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle DEF)}{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)} = k^2 = \dfrac{9}{4}Area(△ABC)Area(△DEF)​=k2=49​

Step 3: Area of △DEF=94×32=72\triangle DEF = \dfrac{9}{4} \times 32 = 72△DEF=49​×32=72 cm²

Q: △PQR∼△XYZ\triangle PQR \sim \triangle XYZ△PQR∼△XYZ. Area of △XYZ=49\triangle XYZ = 49△XYZ=49 cm², area of △PQR=16\triangle PQR = 16△PQR=16 cm². If PQ=8PQ = 8PQ=8 cm, find XYXYXY.

Step 1 — Find k² from areas:

Area(△XYZ)Area(△PQR)=4916\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle XYZ)}{\text{Area}(\triangle PQR)} = \dfrac{49}{16}Area(△PQR)Area(△XYZ)​=1649​

Step 2 — Find k:

k2=4916k^2 = \dfrac{49}{16}k2=1649​ → k=74k = \dfrac{7}{4}k=47​

Step 3 — Find XY:

XYPQ=k\dfrac{XY}{PQ} = kPQXY​=k → XY=k×PQ=74×8=14XY = k \times PQ = \dfrac{7}{4} \times 8 = 14XY=k×PQ=47​×8=14 cm

Q: In △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC, DE∥BCDE \parallel BCDE∥BC, ADAB=13\dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{1}{3}ABAD​=31​. Find the ratio of area of △ADE\triangle ADE△ADE to area of trapezium BCEDBCEDBCED.

Step 1: △ADE∼△ABC\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC△ADE∼△ABC with k=ADAB=13k = \dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{1}{3}k=ABAD​=31​

Step 2: Area(△ADE)Area(△ABC)=k2=19\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle ADE)}{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)} = k^2 = \dfrac{1}{9}Area(△ABC)Area(△ADE)​=k2=91​

So if Area(△ABC)=9x(\triangle ABC) = 9x(△ABC)=9x, then Area(△ADE)=x(\triangle ADE) = x(△ADE)=x.

Step 3: Area of trapezium BCEDBCEDBCED = Area(△ABC)(\triangle ABC)(△ABC) − Area(△ADE)=9x−x=8x(\triangle ADE) = 9x - x = 8x(△ADE)=9x−x=8x

Step 4: Required ratio =x:8x=1:8= x : 8x = \mathbf{1 : 8}=x:8x=1:8

Common mistakes​

Watch out for these

  1. Using k instead of k² for area — the most common error

  2. Forgetting to square the ratio when the ratio of sides is given as a fraction:

    • Side ratio =2:3= 2:3=2:3
    • Area ratio =4:9= 4:9=4:9 (NOT 2:32:32:3!)

  3. Taking square root of area ratio to find side ratio — this IS correct:

    • Area ratio =9:25= 9:25=9:25 → side ratio =3:5= 3:5=3:5 (take \sqrt{}​)

  4. Mixing up which is k and which is 1/k:

    • If smaller triangle has area 4 and larger has area 9, then k=3/2k = 3/2k=3/2 (larger÷smaller for corresponding sides)

Summary​
