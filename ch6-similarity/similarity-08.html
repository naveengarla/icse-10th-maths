8. Maps and Models — Real-World Similarity

Everything is similar!​

A map of India and India itself are similar figures — same shape, different size.

A model car and the real car are similar objects — same proportions, different size.

The scale factor kkk connects the model to the real thing.

The scale factor in maps​

Reading a map scale

A scale of 1 : 50,000 means:

1 unit on the map = 50,000 of the same unit in reality.

So 1 cm on map = 50,000 cm = 500 m = 0.5 km in reality.

The scale factor here is k=50,000k = 50{,}000k=50,000 — the real object is kkk times larger than the map.

Q: On a map with scale 1:25,000, two towns are 4 cm apart. Find the actual distance in km.

Step 1: k=25,000k = 25{,}000k=25,000 (1 cm = 25,000 cm real)

Step 2: Actual distance =4×25,000=100,000= 4 \times 25{,}000 = 100{,}000=4×25,000=100,000 cm =1= 1=1 km

Q: Two cities are 36 km apart. On a map with scale 1 cm = 12 km, find the distance on the map.

Step 1: k=12k = 12k=12 km per cm

Step 2: Map distance =3612=3= \dfrac{36}{12} = 3=1236​=3 cm

Q: A park appears as 6 cm² on a map with scale 1:5000. Find the actual area of the park.

Step 1: k=5000k = 5000k=5000

Step 2: Actual area =6×k2=6×50002=6×25,000,000=150,000,000= 6 \times k^2 = 6 \times 5000^2 = 6 \times 25{,}000{,}000 = 150{,}000{,}000=6×k2=6×50002=6×25,000,000=150,000,000 cm²

Step 3: Convert: 150,000,000150{,}000{,}000150,000,000 cm² =15,000= 15{,}000=15,000 m² =1.5= 1.5=1.5 hectares

Q: A model of a building is made at scale 1:100.

  • Height of model = 30 cm. Find real height.
  • Area of model's roof = 50 cm². Find real roof area.
  • Volume of model = 200 cm³. Find real volume.

Scale factor: k=100k = 100k=100

Real height =30×100=3000= 30 \times 100 = 3000=30×100=3000 cm =30= 30=30 m ✓

Real roof area =50×1002=500,000= 50 \times 100^2 = 500{,}000=50×1002=500,000 cm² =50= 50=50 m²

Real volume =200×1003=2×108= 200 \times 100^3 = 2 \times 10^8=200×1003=2×108 cm³
