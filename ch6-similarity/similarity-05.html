5. Finding Corresponding Sides — The Hardest Skill

Why this trips everyone up​

You now know when two triangles are similar (AA, SAS, SSS).

But the harder question is: once you know they're similar, which side of one triangle corresponds to which side of the other?

This is where most students lose marks in ICSE — they set up the wrong ratio and get the wrong answer.

The problem is worse when triangles are:

  • Rotated (one is turned relative to the other)
  • Flipped (one is a mirror image)
  • Inside the same figure (both triangles share vertices or sides)

The key principle​

The one rule that fixes everything

Corresponding sides are OPPOSITE corresponding (equal) angles.

If ∠A=∠P\angle A = \angle P∠A=∠P, then side BCBCBC (opposite ∠A\angle A∠A) corresponds to side QRQRQR (opposite ∠P\angle P∠P).

This means: to find which sides correspond, find which angles are equal first.

Step-by-step method​

Given △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR (letters in this order):

In △ABC\triangle ABC△ABCOpposite angleCorresponds toIn △PQR\triangle PQR△PQR
Side BCBCBC∠A\angle A∠A↔Side QRQRQR (opposite ∠P\angle P∠P)
Side CACACA∠B\angle B∠B↔Side RPRPRP (opposite ∠Q\angle Q∠Q)
Side ABABAB∠C\angle C∠C↔Side PQPQPQ (opposite ∠R\angle R∠R)

The proportion is: BCQR=CARP=ABPQ=k\dfrac{BC}{QR} = \dfrac{CA}{RP} = \dfrac{AB}{PQ} = kQRBC​=RPCA​=PQAB​=k

The shortcut: In △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR, the letters tell you the correspondence directly:

  • 1st letter A ↔ 1st letter P
  • 2nd letter B ↔ 2nd letter Q
  • 3rd letter C ↔ 3rd letter R

The rotation problem — visually​

This is the most common trap. Two triangles that are similar but one is rotated or flipped. The angle arc colours tell you which vertices match.

The trick: colour your angle arcs before writing any ratio.

The "triangle inside a triangle" figure​

This is the most common ICSE question type for similarity.

Pattern: A line parallel to the base of a triangle cuts the other two sides, creating a smaller similar triangle inside.

The golden rule: In △CDE∼△CAB\triangle CDE \sim \triangle CAB△CDE∼△CAB: CDCA=DEAB=CECB\dfrac{CD}{CA} = \dfrac{DE}{AB} = \dfrac{CE}{CB}CACD​=ABDE​=CBCE​

Note: it's CD/CACD/CACD/CA NOT CD/ADCD/ADCD/AD. The ratio is the WHOLE side to the WHOLE side — because △CDE\triangle CDE△CDE corresponds to △CAB\triangle CAB△CAB, and CACACA is the full side.

The three most common mistake patterns​

  • Mistake 1 — Wrong order
  • Mistake 2 — Partial side
  • Mistake 3 — Mixed triangles

Wrong: △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR → writing ABQR=BCPQ\dfrac{AB}{QR} = \dfrac{BC}{PQ}QRAB​=PQBC​

Why it's wrong: ABABAB is opposite ∠C\angle C∠C and QRQRQR is opposite ∠P\angle P∠P. These angles are not equal.

Correct: ABPQ=BCQR\dfrac{AB}{PQ} = \dfrac{BC}{QR}PQAB​=QRBC​ (match 1st with 1st, 2nd with 2nd)

Figure: DDD on ABABAB, line DE∥BCDE \parallel BCDE∥BC, so △ADE∼△ABC\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC△ADE∼△ABC.

Wrong: ADDB=AEBC\dfrac{AD}{DB} = \dfrac{AE}{BC}DBAD​=BCAE​ — mixing DBDBDB (a part) with BCBCBC (a whole side)

Correct: ADAB=AEAC=DEBC\dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{AE}{AC} = \dfrac{DE}{BC}ABAD​=ACAE​=BCDE​ — always use the FULL corresponding sides

Wrong: Writing ratios with one side from each triangle in the same fraction position

For △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR:

ABQR\dfrac{AB}{QR}QRAB​ — this mixes: ABABAB is opposite ∠C\angle C∠C, QRQRQR is opposite ∠P\angle P∠P. If ∠C≠∠P\angle C \neq \angle P∠C=∠P, this ratio means nothing.

Correct: Keep all sides from △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC on top (or all on bottom): ABPQ=BCQR\dfrac{AB}{PQ} = \dfrac{BC}{QR}PQAB​=QRBC​

Question: In △ABC\triangle ABC△ABC, DE∥BCDE \parallel BCDE∥BC where DDD is on ABABAB and EEE is on ACACAC. AD=3AD = 3AD=3, DB=5DB = 5DB=5, AE=4AE = 4AE=4. Find ECECEC and DEDEDE if BC=12BC = 12BC=12.

Step 1 — Prove similar:

△ADE∼△ABC\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC△ADE∼△ABC (AA — common angle at AAA, corresponding angles from DE∥BCDE \parallel BCDE∥BC)

Step 2 — Set up ratio: (using full sides)

ADAB=AEAC=DEBC\dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{AE}{AC} = \dfrac{DE}{BC}ABAD​=ACAE​=BCDE​

33+5=44+EC=DE12\dfrac{3}{3+5} = \dfrac{4}{4+EC} = \dfrac{DE}{12}3+53​=4+EC4​=12DE​

Step 3 — Find EC:

38=44+EC  ⟹  3(4+EC)=32  ⟹  EC=203≈6.67\dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{4}{4+EC} \implies 3(4+EC) = 32 \implies EC = \dfrac{20}{3} \approx 6.6783​=4+EC4​⟹3(4+EC)=32⟹EC=320​≈6.67

Step 4 — Find DE:

38=DE12  ⟹  DE=368=4.5\dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{DE}{12} \implies DE = \dfrac{36}{8} = 4.583​=12DE​⟹DE=836​=4.5

Question: In the figure, ∠ABC=∠ACD\angle ABC = \angle ACD∠ABC=∠ACD. AB=8AB = 8AB=8, BC=6BC = 6BC=6, AC=4AC = 4AC=4. Find CDCDCD.

Step 1 — Find common angle: ∠A=∠A\angle A = \angle A∠A=∠A (shared)

Step 2 — Two equal angles (AA): △ABC∼△ACD\triangle ABC \sim \triangle ACD△ABC∼△ACD

(Careful: ∠A\angle A∠A is first in both, ∠ABC\angle ABC∠ABC matches ∠ACD\angle ACD∠ACD)

Step 3 — Write ratio (A↔A, B↔C, C↔D):

ABAC=BCCD=ACAD\dfrac{AB}{AC} = \dfrac{BC}{CD} = \dfrac{AC}{AD}ACAB​=CDBC​=ADAC​

84=6CD\dfrac{8}{4} = \dfrac{6}{CD}48​=CD6​

CD=6×48=3CD = \dfrac{6 \times 4}{8} = 3CD=86×4​=3

Question: Diagonals of trapezium ABCDABCDABCD meet at OOO (where AB∥CDAB \parallel CDAB∥CD). Prove △AOB∼△COD\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD△AOB∼△COD.

Step 1: ∠AOB=∠COD\angle AOB = \angle COD∠AOB=∠COD (vertically opposite angles)

Step 2: ∠OAB=∠OCD\angle OAB = \angle OCD∠OAB=∠OCD (alternate angles, AB∥CDAB \parallel CDAB∥CD)

Step 3: △AOB∼△COD\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD△AOB∼△COD by AA

Corresponding sides:

OAOC=OBOD=ABCD\dfrac{OA}{OC} = \dfrac{OB}{OD} = \dfrac{AB}{CD}OCOA​=ODOB​=CDAB​

The four-step method for any similarity problem​

Exam method — use this every time

Step 1: Identify the two triangles being compared (name them clearly)

Step 2: Mark equal angles in the figure (use arc symbols or colour coding in your head)

Step 3: Write the similarity statement with letters in MATCHING ORDER

Step 4: Write the proportion side1side1′=side2side2′=side3side3′\dfrac{\text{side}_1}{\text{side}_1'} = \dfrac{\text{side}_2}{\text{side}_2'} = \dfrac{\text{side}_3}{\text{side}_3'}side1′​side1​​=side2′​side2​​=side3′​side3​​ and solve

Summary​

What to remember
  1. Corresponding sides are opposite equal angles
  2. Letter order matters: △ABC∼△PQR\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR△ABC∼△PQR means A↔P, B↔Q, C↔R
  3. Always use complete sides — not partial segments (unless the question asks about segments)
  4. In a figure: mark angle arcs with different symbols before writing any ratio
  5. Most common figure type: parallel line inside triangle → AA similarity
