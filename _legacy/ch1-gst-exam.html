Selling Price
SP = MP × (100−d)/100
d = discount %
GST Amount
GST = r/100 × SP
r = GST rate %
Final Bill
Bill = SP × (100+r)/100
One-step multiplier
Intra-State Split
CGST = SGST = r/2 %
each applied to SP
One-Step Master Formula
Bill = MP × (100−d)/100 × (100+r)/100
Combines discount and GST in one shot
Price Before GST (from inclusive)
SP = A × 100/(100+r)
A = given inclusive price
GST Inside Inclusive Price
GST = A × r/(100+r)
or: A − SP
Find MP (given bill, d%, r%)
MP = Bill ÷ [(100−d)/100] ÷ [(100+r)/100]
Divide out both multipliers
Inter-State
IGST = Full GST Rate
No CGST/SGST split
Net GST Payable
Net = Output Tax − ITC
ITC = input tax already paid
Shortcut (same rate)
Net = (r/100) × (SP − CP)
Only when buy & sell rates match
Output Tax
Output = r/100 × SP
Input Tax
Input = r/100 × CP
GST Rate Change
Change = Base × (r₁−r₂)/100
Base = pre-tax price
Successive Discounts
Eq. = d₁+d₂ − d₁d₂/100
Never simply add d₁+d₂
Successive Discounts — Apply Step-by-Step
Price = MP × (1−d₁/100) × (1−d₂/100)
⚠️ Top 6 Traps
TRAP 1 — GST on SP, not MP

Wrong: 18% × ₹40,000. Right: 18% × ₹36,000.

TRAP 2 — Successive Discounts

10% + 5% ≠ 15%. Apply one by one: 10,000 × 0.9 × 0.95.

TRAP 3 — IGST vs CGST+SGST

Inter-state → IGST only. Intra-state → CGST + SGST. Never both.

TRAP 4 — Reverse GST

If price includes 12% GST: SP = Price × 100/112.
Wrong: SP = Price − 12% of Price (circular).

TRAP 5 — ITC Confusion

Net GST = Output Tax − Input Tax Credit.
Consumer gets NO ITC.

TRAP 6 — Profit Excludes GST

Profit = SP (pre-GST) − CP (pre-GST). GST is never profit.

Worked Examples — ICSE Board Style
Q1 — Discount + CGST + SGST
MOST COMMON
TV: MP ₹45,000, 15% discount, 28% GST intra-state. Find SP, CGST, SGST, Bill.
①SP = 45,000 × 85/100 = ₹38,250
②CGST = SGST = 14% → 14/100 × 38,250 = ₹5,355 each
③Bill = 38,250 + 5,355 + 5,355 = ₹48,960
SP = ₹38,250 | CGST = SGST = ₹5,355 | Bill = ₹48,960
Q2 — Inclusive Price (Reverse)
FREQUENTLY ASKED
Customer pays ₹5,900 including 18% GST. Find original price and GST amount.
①₹5,900 = 118% of SP → SP = 5900 × 100/118 = ₹5,000
②GST = 5,900 − 5,000 = ₹900
Original Price = ₹5,000 | GST = ₹900
Q3 — Net GST / ITC
SUPPLY CHAIN
Wholesaler: buys ₹40,000, sells ₹55,000. GST 12%. Find net GST payable and invoice to retailer.
①Input Tax = 12% × 40,000 = ₹4,800
②Output Tax = 12% × 55,000 = ₹6,600
③Net GST = 6,600 − 4,800 = ₹1,800
④Invoice = 55,000 + 6,600 = ₹61,600
Net GST = ₹1,800 | Invoice = ₹61,600
Q4 — Find Marked Price
FULL REVERSE
Customer pays ₹28,320 after 20% discount + 18% GST. Find MP.
①Let MP = x. SP = x × 80/100
②Bill = SP × 118/100 = x × 80/100 × 118/100 = 28,320
③x = 28,320 × 10000/9440 = ₹30,000
Marked Price = ₹30,000
Q5 — Successive Discounts + GST
TRAP TYPE
MP ₹10,000, discounts 10% and 5% successively, GST 12%. Find bill.
①After 1st: 10,000 × 90/100 = ₹9,000
②After 2nd: 9,000 × 95/100 = ₹8,550
③Bill: 8,550 × 112/100 = ₹9,576
Bill = ₹9,576
Q6 — GST Rate Change
RATE CHANGE
Pre-tax price ₹30,000. GST drops 28% → 18%. How much cheaper for consumer?
①Shortcut: 30,000 × (28−18)/100 = ₹3,000
✓Or: Old ₹38,400 − New ₹35,400 = ₹3,000
Price reduced by ₹3,000

Memory Hooks
💡 "Discount first, Tax last"

Discount → Taxable Value → GST on top. GST never touches the discount.

💡 IGST = Inter-state Goes via Centre

Same state → CGST+SGST. Different state → IGST only.

💡 Reverse: × 100/(100+r)

Peel GST off an inclusive price: multiply by 100/(100+r).

💡 Net GST = Tax on your Value-Add

Dealer pays GST only on what they added (SP − CP), not the whole SP.

Pre-Exam Checklist
  • CGST = SGST = GST rate ÷ 2 (intra-state)
  • GST always on SP (after discount), never on MP
  • Successive discounts: apply one by one, never add
  • Reverse: SP = Inclusive × 100/(100+r)
  • Net GST = Output Tax − Input Tax Credit
  • Inter-state = IGST only; never with CGST/SGST
  • Consumer bears full GST, no ITC available to them
  • Profit calculated on pre-GST prices
  • GST rate change: Change = Base × Δr/100
  • Multi-item bill: GST per item, then grand total
