📦 The Story — Why does GST exist?
🎮 Finland Phenomenon — Arjun's Gaming Rig

Arjun walks into a Delhi electronics shop to buy a graphics card for his PC. The box has a sticker: ₹40,000 (Marked Price). The shopkeeper offers a 10% festival discount. The government requires 18% GST on electronics. Delhi → same state seller → intra-state transaction.

How much does Arjun actually pay? Where does the tax money go? Why is the tax not on ₹40,000? — Every formula you learn in this chapter answers one of these three questions.

Before 2017, India had many separate taxes — VAT, Excise Duty, Service Tax, Entry Tax — each charged differently by Centre and State. A product could be taxed 5–6 times on its journey from factory to consumer. GST replaced all of these with one unified tax collected at each stage.

📋 DIRECT TAX

Paid directly by the earner to the government.
Example: Income Tax.

🛒 INDIRECT TAX (GST)

Collected from the consumer through the seller. The seller is a tax agent for the government.

Step 1 — Discount

Discount = d% × M.P. ÷ 100

Arjun: 10% × 40,000 ÷ 100 = ₹4,000

Step 2 — Selling Price

S.P. = M.P. × (100−d) ÷ 100

Arjun: 40,000 × 90/100 = ₹36,000

Step 3 — GST

GST = r% × S.P. ÷ 100

Arjun: 18% × 36,000 ÷ 100 = ₹6,480

Step 4 — Final Bill

Bill = S.P. × (100 + r) ÷ 100

Arjun: 36,000 × 118/100 = ₹42,480

⚠️ THE #1 EXAM TRAP

GST rate applies to S.P. (after discount), never to M.P. If you get ₹7,200 (= 18% of ₹40,000) instead of ₹6,480 (= 18% of ₹36,000) — you fell for it.

🗺️ CGST vs SGST vs IGST
Rule: Intra-state → CGST + SGST. Each = Total GST Rate ÷ 2.
At 18%: CGST = 9%, SGST = 9%.
Rule: Inter-state → only IGST. IGST rate = Total GST Rate. No split into CGST/SGST.
⚠️ Never mix them

A transaction cannot have BOTH IGST and CGST/SGST. It's one or the other, based purely on whether states match.

🗣️ Formulas in 4 Languages (Finland Languaging)

Finland's research shows that a student owns a formula only when they can express it in 4 different ways. Use the tabs to work through all four for each formula.

Formula 1 — Standard Bill (MP → Discount → GST → Bill)

Subtract the discount from the Marked Price to get the Selling Price. The government then adds a percentage on top of only this Selling Price. The customer pays the Selling Price plus the tax.

MP → chop off discount → SP → glue on GST → BILL
(Refer to the bar model in Section 3)
SP = MP × (100−d)/100
Bill = SP × (100+r)/100
Bill = MP × (100−d)/100 × (100+r)/100

This one-step formula gives the final bill directly.

Practice: MP = ₹5,000, Discount = 20%, GST = 12%. Find the bill.

SP = 5,000 × 80/100 = ₹4,000
GST = 12% × 4,000 = ₹480
Bill = 4,000 + 480 = ₹4,480

Formula 2 — Reverse: Given Inclusive Price, Find SP and GST

If the price you're given already has GST baked in, it equals (100 + GST%) of the original price. To strip the GST out, divide by (100 + r) and multiply by 100.

Given price A = (100+r)% of SP.
∴ SP = A ÷ (100+r) × 100 = "peeling off the GST layer"

Think: if 118 rupees includes 18% GST, then 100 of those rupees are the actual price and 18 are the tax.
SP = A × 100 / (100 + r)
GST in A = A × r / (100 + r)

A = the inclusive (given) price, r = GST rate %

Practice: A customer pays ₹2,360 including 18% GST. Find original price and GST.

SP = 2360 × 100/118 = ₹2,000
GST = 2360 − 2000 = ₹360

Formula 3 — Net GST Payable (for Dealers)

A dealer collects GST from customers (output tax) but also paid GST when buying stock (input tax). The government only wants the difference — the tax on the dealer's "value added".

OUTPUT TAX (GST collected from buyer)
− INPUT TAX CREDIT (GST paid to supplier)
= NET GST PAYABLE to government
Net GST = Output Tax − Input Tax Credit
Net GST = (r/100) × (SP − CP) [same rate only]

The shortcut works only when the GST rate on buying and selling is the same.

Practice: Dealer buys at ₹20,000, sells at ₹28,000. GST = 12%. Find net GST payable.

Output Tax = 12% × 28,000 = ₹3,360
Input Tax = 12% × 20,000 = ₹2,400
Net GST = 3,360 − 2,400 = ₹960
Shortcut: 12% × (28,000−20,000) = 12% × 8,000 = ₹960 ✓
⛓️ Supply Chain & Input Tax Credit

Each dealer pays GST on their selling price but claims back what they paid. The consumer bears the full burden — no ITC for them.

Key Insight: The government collects the same total tax (₹6,480) regardless of how many stages there are. Each dealer forwards only their piece — the tax on what they added.
✏️ Worked Examples

Q1 — Discount + CGST + SGST (Standard)

MOST COMMON
A television is marked at ₹45,000. A 15% discount is given. GST = 28% (intra-state). Find (i) S.P. (ii) CGST (iii) SGST (iv) Amount paid by buyer.
1
S.P. = 45,000 × (100−15)/100 = 45,000 × 85/100 = ₹38,250
2
CGST Rate = SGST Rate = 28/2 = 14% each
3
CGST = SGST = 14/100 × 38,250 = ₹5,355 each
4
Bill = 38,250 + 5,355 + 5,355 = ₹48,960
✓ S.P. = ₹38,250 | CGST = ₹5,355 | SGST = ₹5,355 | Bill = ₹48,960

Q2 — Successive Discounts + GST

TRAP TYPE
MP = ₹10,000. Two discounts of 10% and 5% successively. GST = 12%. Find the bill.
1
After 1st discount: 10,000 × 90/100 = ₹9,000
2
After 2nd discount: 9,000 × 95/100 = ₹8,550 (taxable value)
3
Bill = 8,550 × 112/100 = ₹9,576
⚠️ Why not 15%?

10% + 5% = 14.5% equivalent (not 15%). Direct formula: d₁+d₂ − d₁d₂/100 = 10+5−50/100 = 14.5%.

✓ Bill = ₹9,576

Q3 — Price Inclusive of GST

REVERSE TYPE
Priya pays ₹7,080 for a saree that includes 18% GST. Find the price before GST and the GST amount.
1
₹7,080 represents 118% of the selling price (100% + 18%).
2
S.P. = 7,080 × 100/118 = ₹6,000
3
GST = 7,080 − 6,000 = ₹1,080 (or: 7,080 × 18/118)
✓ Price before GST = ₹6,000 | GST = ₹1,080

Q4 — Find Marked Price from Bill

FULL REVERSE
A customer pays ₹28,320 for a camera after a 20% discount and 18% GST. Find the Marked Price.
1
Let MP = x. After 20% discount: SP = x × 80/100
2
After 18% GST: Bill = SP × 118/100
3
x × 80/100 × 118/100 = 28,320
4
x × 9440/10000 = 28,320 → x = 28,320 × 10000/9440 = ₹30,000
✓ Marked Price = ₹30,000

Q5 — Net GST Payable by Dealer

ITC TYPE
A wholesaler buys goods for ₹50,000 and sells them for ₹65,000. GST = 12% (intra-state). Find: (i) Input Tax (ii) Output Tax (iii) Net GST payable (iv) Amount received from retailer.
1
Input Tax = 12/100 × 50,000 = ₹6,000
2
Output Tax = 12/100 × 65,000 = ₹7,800
3
Net GST = 7,800 − 6,000 = ₹1,800
Shortcut: 12% × (65,000−50,000) = 12% × 15,000 = ₹1,800 ✓
4
Invoice to retailer = 65,000 + 7,800 = ₹72,800
✓ Input Tax = ₹6,000 | Output Tax = ₹7,800 | Net GST = ₹1,800 | Invoice = ₹72,800

Q6 — GST Rate Reduction

RATE CHANGE
The pre-tax price of an AC is ₹30,000. GST falls from 28% to 18%. By how much does the consumer's price change?
1
Old Bill = 30,000 × 128/100 = ₹38,400
2
New Bill = 30,000 × 118/100 = ₹35,400
3
Reduction = 38,400 − 35,400 = ₹3,000
✓
Shortcut: Base × (r₁−r₂)/100 = 30,000 × 10/100 = ₹3,000
✓ Consumer price reduced by ₹3,000
