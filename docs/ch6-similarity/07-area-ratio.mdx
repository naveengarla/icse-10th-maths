# 7. Areas of Similar Triangles

## The surprising result

You already know: if two triangles are similar with scale factor $k$, every side is multiplied by $k$.

Here is the result that surprises almost every student:

:::note Area Ratio Theorem
If $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ with scale factor $k$, then:

$$\dfrac{\text{Area of } \triangle PQR}{\text{Area of } \triangle ABC} = k^2$$

The area ratio is the **square** of the linear scale factor.
:::

Side ratio = $k$. Area ratio = $k^2$. **NOT** $k$.

---

## Why k² and not k? — The visual proof

<AreaRatioSquares />

The key insight: **area is two-dimensional**. When you scale both length AND width by $k$, the area scales by $k \times k = k^2$.

A triangle is just like the square — it has two dimensions. Double every side → area becomes $2 \times 2 = 4$ times larger.

## The formula — different forms

$$\dfrac{\text{Area}_1}{\text{Area}_2} = \left(\dfrac{\text{corresponding side}_1}{\text{corresponding side}_2}\right)^2 = k^2$$

This can be rewritten with any corresponding measurement:

$$\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)}{\text{Area}(\triangle PQR)} = \left(\dfrac{AB}{PQ}\right)^2 = \left(\dfrac{BC}{QR}\right)^2 = \left(\dfrac{CA}{RP}\right)^2$$

## Worked examples

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="ex1" label="Find area ratio">

**Q:** $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ with $AB = 6$ and $PQ = 9$. Find the ratio of their areas.

**Step 1 — Find k:** $k = \dfrac{PQ}{AB} = \dfrac{9}{6} = \dfrac{3}{2}$

**Step 2 — Area ratio:** $\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle PQR)}{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)} = k^2 = \left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{9}{4}$

**Answer:** Area of $\triangle PQR$ : Area of $\triangle ABC$ = $9 : 4$

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="ex2" label="Find unknown area">

**Q:** $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF$. $AB = 4$, $DE = 6$, Area of $\triangle ABC = 32$ cm². Find area of $\triangle DEF$.

**Step 1:** $k = \dfrac{DE}{AB} = \dfrac{6}{4} = \dfrac{3}{2}$

**Step 2:** $\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle DEF)}{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)} = k^2 = \dfrac{9}{4}$

**Step 3:** Area of $\triangle DEF = \dfrac{9}{4} \times 32 = 72$ cm²

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="ex3" label="Find k from areas">

**Q:** $\triangle PQR \sim \triangle XYZ$. Area of $\triangle XYZ = 49$ cm², area of $\triangle PQR = 16$ cm². If $PQ = 8$ cm, find $XY$.

**Step 1 — Find k² from areas:**

$\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle XYZ)}{\text{Area}(\triangle PQR)} = \dfrac{49}{16}$

**Step 2 — Find k:**

$k^2 = \dfrac{49}{16}$ → $k = \dfrac{7}{4}$

**Step 3 — Find XY:**

$\dfrac{XY}{PQ} = k$ → $XY = k \times PQ = \dfrac{7}{4} \times 8 = 14$ cm

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="ex4" label="Ratio of remaining area">

**Q:** In $\triangle ABC$, $DE \parallel BC$, $\dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{1}{3}$. Find the ratio of area of $\triangle ADE$ to area of trapezium $BCED$.

**Step 1:** $\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC$ with $k = \dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{1}{3}$

**Step 2:** $\dfrac{\text{Area}(\triangle ADE)}{\text{Area}(\triangle ABC)} = k^2 = \dfrac{1}{9}$

So if Area$(\triangle ABC) = 9x$, then Area$(\triangle ADE) = x$.

**Step 3:** Area of trapezium $BCED$ = Area$(\triangle ABC)$ − Area$(\triangle ADE) = 9x - x = 8x$

**Step 4:** Required ratio $= x : 8x = \mathbf{1 : 8}$

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Common mistakes

:::warning Watch out for these
1. **Using k instead of k²** for area — the most common error

2. **Forgetting to square the ratio** when the ratio of sides is given as a fraction:
   - Side ratio $= 2:3$
   - Area ratio $= 4:9$ (NOT $2:3$!)

3. **Taking square root of area ratio to find side ratio** — this IS correct:
   - Area ratio $= 9:25$ → side ratio $= 3:5$ (take $\sqrt{}$)

4. **Mixing up which is k and which is 1/k**:
   - If smaller triangle has area 4 and larger has area 9, then $k = 3/2$ (larger÷smaller for corresponding sides)
:::

## Summary

:::tip Area Ratio — one formula to know
$$\dfrac{\text{Area}_1}{\text{Area}_2} = \left(\dfrac{\text{side}_1}{\text{side}_2}\right)^2 = k^2$$

To reverse: $\dfrac{\text{side}_1}{\text{side}_2} = \sqrt{\dfrac{\text{Area}_1}{\text{Area}_2}}$
:::
