# 8. Maps and Models — Real-World Similarity

## Everything is similar!

A map of India and India itself are **similar figures** — same shape, different size.

A model car and the real car are **similar objects** — same proportions, different size.

The **scale factor** $k$ connects the model to the real thing.

---

## The scale factor in maps

<MapScaleSVG />

:::note Reading a map scale
A scale of **1 : 50,000** means:

1 unit on the map = 50,000 of the same unit in reality.

So 1 cm on map = 50,000 cm = **500 m** = **0.5 km** in reality.
:::

The scale factor here is $k = 50{,}000$ — the real object is $k$ times larger than the map.

---

## What scales by what

<ScaleTable />

This table is the key to all map/model problems:

| What you're finding | Multiply map/model value by |
|---------------------|----------------------------|
| Length / distance / perimeter | $k$ |
| Area | $k^2$ |
| Volume (for 3D models) | $k^3$ |

---

## How to find k from a given scale

| Scale notation | What it means | k |
|---------------|--------------|---|
| 1 : 200 | 1 unit on model = 200 real units | k = 200 |
| 1 cm represents 5 km | 1 cm = 500,000 cm | k = 500,000 |
| 1 : 1000 | Model is 1/1000 of real size | k = 1000 |

**Finding k from measurements:**

$$k = \dfrac{\text{actual length}}{\text{map/model length}}$$

---

## Worked examples

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="length" label="Find actual length">

**Q:** On a map with scale 1:25,000, two towns are 4 cm apart. Find the actual distance in km.

**Step 1:** $k = 25{,}000$ (1 cm = 25,000 cm real)

**Step 2:** Actual distance $= 4 \times 25{,}000 = 100{,}000$ cm $= 1$ km

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="map-length" label="Find map length">

**Q:** Two cities are 36 km apart. On a map with scale 1 cm = 12 km, find the distance on the map.

**Step 1:** $k = 12$ km per cm

**Step 2:** Map distance $= \dfrac{36}{12} = 3$ cm

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="area" label="Find actual area">

**Q:** A park appears as 6 cm² on a map with scale 1:5000. Find the actual area of the park.

**Step 1:** $k = 5000$

**Step 2:** Actual area $= 6 \times k^2 = 6 \times 5000^2 = 6 \times 25{,}000{,}000 = 150{,}000{,}000$ cm²

**Step 3:** Convert: $150{,}000{,}000$ cm² $= 15{,}000$ m² $= 1.5$ hectares

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="model" label="Model problem">

**Q:** A model of a building is made at scale 1:100.
- Height of model = 30 cm. Find real height.
- Area of model's roof = 50 cm². Find real roof area.
- Volume of model = 200 cm³. Find real volume.

**Scale factor:** $k = 100$

Real height $= 30 \times 100 = 3000$ cm $= 30$ m ✓

Real roof area $= 50 \times 100^2 = 500{,}000$ cm² $= 50$ m²

Real volume $= 200 \times 100^3 = 2 \times 10^8$ cm³

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Finding the scale factor from measurements

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="from-map" label="From map measurement">

**Q:** On a map, a road is 3 cm long. The actual road is 15 km long. Find the scale of the map.

$$k = \dfrac{\text{actual}}{\text{map}} = \dfrac{15 \text{ km}}{3 \text{ cm}} = \dfrac{15 \times 100{,}000 \text{ cm}}{3 \text{ cm}} = 500{,}000$$

Scale = **1 : 5,00,000**

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="from-areas" label="From area measurements">

**Q:** The floor area of a room is 20 m². On the architect's plan, it appears as 5 cm². Find the scale factor.

$$k^2 = \dfrac{\text{actual area}}{\text{plan area}} = \dfrac{20 \text{ m}^2}{5 \text{ cm}^2} = \dfrac{20 \times 10{,}000 \text{ cm}^2}{5 \text{ cm}^2} = 40{,}000$$

$$k = \sqrt{40{,}000} = 200$$

Scale = **1 : 200**

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Unit conversions to remember

| Length | Area |
|--------|------|
| 1 km = 1000 m = 100,000 cm | 1 m = 100 cm |
| 1 km² = 1,000,000 m² | 1 m² = 10,000 cm² |
| 1 hectare = 10,000 m² | 1 km² = 100 hectares |

## Summary

:::tip Maps and Models — the full picture
$$k = \dfrac{\text{actual size}}{\text{map/model size}}$$

| Dimension | Scales by |
|-----------|----------|
| Length, distance, perimeter | $k$ |
| Area | $k^2$ |
| Volume | $k^3$ |

**Always convert to same units before computing k!**
:::
