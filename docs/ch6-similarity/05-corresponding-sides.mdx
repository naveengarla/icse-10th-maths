# 5. Finding Corresponding Sides — The Hardest Skill

## Why this trips everyone up

You now know *when* two triangles are similar (AA, SAS, SSS).

But the harder question is: **once you know they're similar, which side of one triangle corresponds to which side of the other?**

This is where most students lose marks in ICSE — they set up the wrong ratio and get the wrong answer.

The problem is worse when triangles are:
- **Rotated** (one is turned relative to the other)
- **Flipped** (one is a mirror image)
- **Inside the same figure** (both triangles share vertices or sides)

---

## The key principle

:::note The one rule that fixes everything
**Corresponding sides are OPPOSITE corresponding (equal) angles.**

If $\angle A = \angle P$, then side $BC$ (opposite $\angle A$) corresponds to side $QR$ (opposite $\angle P$).
:::

This means: to find which sides correspond, **find which angles are equal first**.

---

## Step-by-step method

**Given $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ (letters in this order):**

| In $\triangle ABC$ | Opposite angle | Corresponds to | In $\triangle PQR$ |
|-------------------|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Side $BC$ | $\angle A$ | ↔ | Side $QR$ (opposite $\angle P$) |
| Side $CA$ | $\angle B$ | ↔ | Side $RP$ (opposite $\angle Q$) |
| Side $AB$ | $\angle C$ | ↔ | Side $PQ$ (opposite $\angle R$) |

The proportion is: $\dfrac{BC}{QR} = \dfrac{CA}{RP} = \dfrac{AB}{PQ} = k$

**The shortcut:** In $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$, the letters tell you the correspondence directly:
- 1st letter A ↔ 1st letter P
- 2nd letter B ↔ 2nd letter Q
- 3rd letter C ↔ 3rd letter R

## The "triangle inside a triangle" figure

This is the most common ICSE question type for similarity.

<TriangleInsideTriangle />

**Pattern:** A line parallel to the base of a triangle cuts the other two sides, creating a smaller similar triangle inside.

**The golden rule:** In $\triangle CDE \sim \triangle CAB$:
$$\dfrac{CD}{CA} = \dfrac{DE}{AB} = \dfrac{CE}{CB}$$

Note: it's $CD/CA$ NOT $CD/AD$. The ratio is the WHOLE side to the WHOLE side — because $\triangle CDE$ corresponds to $\triangle CAB$, and $CA$ is the full side.

## The three most common mistake patterns

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="m1" label="Mistake 1 — Wrong order">

**Wrong:** $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ → writing $\dfrac{AB}{QR} = \dfrac{BC}{PQ}$

**Why it's wrong:** $AB$ is opposite $\angle C$ and $QR$ is opposite $\angle P$. These angles are not equal.

**Correct:** $\dfrac{AB}{PQ} = \dfrac{BC}{QR}$ (match 1st with 1st, 2nd with 2nd)

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="m2" label="Mistake 2 — Partial side">

**Figure:** $D$ on $AB$, line $DE \parallel BC$, so $\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC$.

**Wrong:** $\dfrac{AD}{DB} = \dfrac{AE}{BC}$ — mixing $DB$ (a part) with $BC$ (a whole side)

**Correct:** $\dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{AE}{AC} = \dfrac{DE}{BC}$ — always use the FULL corresponding sides

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="m3" label="Mistake 3 — Mixed triangles">

**Wrong:** Writing ratios with one side from each triangle in the *same* fraction position

For $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$:

$\dfrac{AB}{QR}$ — this mixes: $AB$ is opposite $\angle C$, $QR$ is opposite $\angle P$. If $\angle C \neq \angle P$, this ratio means nothing.

**Correct:** Keep all sides from $\triangle ABC$ on top (or all on bottom): $\dfrac{AB}{PQ} = \dfrac{BC}{QR}$

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Worked examples with figures

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="ex1" label="Example 1 — Parallel Line">

**Question:** In $\triangle ABC$, $DE \parallel BC$ where $D$ is on $AB$ and $E$ is on $AC$. $AD = 3$, $DB = 5$, $AE = 4$. Find $EC$ and $DE$ if $BC = 12$.

**Step 1 — Prove similar:**

$\triangle ADE \sim \triangle ABC$ (AA — common angle at $A$, corresponding angles from $DE \parallel BC$)

**Step 2 — Set up ratio:** (using full sides)

$$\dfrac{AD}{AB} = \dfrac{AE}{AC} = \dfrac{DE}{BC}$$

$$\dfrac{3}{3+5} = \dfrac{4}{4+EC} = \dfrac{DE}{12}$$

**Step 3 — Find EC:**

$$\dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{4}{4+EC} \implies 3(4+EC) = 32 \implies EC = \dfrac{20}{3} \approx 6.67$$

**Step 4 — Find DE:**

$$\dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{DE}{12} \implies DE = \dfrac{36}{8} = 4.5$$

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="ex2" label="Example 2 — Rotated Triangles">

**Question:** In the figure, $\angle ABC = \angle ACD$. $AB = 8$, $BC = 6$, $AC = 4$. Find $CD$.

**Step 1 — Find common angle:** $\angle A = \angle A$ (shared)

**Step 2 — Two equal angles (AA):** $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle ACD$

(Careful: $\angle A$ is first in both, $\angle ABC$ matches $\angle ACD$)

**Step 3 — Write ratio** (A↔A, B↔C, C↔D):

$$\dfrac{AB}{AC} = \dfrac{BC}{CD} = \dfrac{AC}{AD}$$

$$\dfrac{8}{4} = \dfrac{6}{CD}$$

$$CD = \dfrac{6 \times 4}{8} = 3$$

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="ex3" label="Example 3 — Two Triangles Meeting at Vertex">

**Question:** Diagonals of trapezium $ABCD$ meet at $O$ (where $AB \parallel CD$). Prove $\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD$.

**Step 1:** $\angle AOB = \angle COD$ (vertically opposite angles)

**Step 2:** $\angle OAB = \angle OCD$ (alternate angles, $AB \parallel CD$)

**Step 3:** $\triangle AOB \sim \triangle COD$ by **AA**

**Corresponding sides:**

$$\dfrac{OA}{OC} = \dfrac{OB}{OD} = \dfrac{AB}{CD}$$

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## The four-step method for any similarity problem

:::tip Exam method — use this every time
**Step 1:** Identify the two triangles being compared (name them clearly)

**Step 2:** Mark equal angles in the figure (use arc symbols or colour coding in your head)

**Step 3:** Write the similarity statement with letters in MATCHING ORDER

**Step 4:** Write the proportion $\dfrac{\text{side}_1}{\text{side}_1'} = \dfrac{\text{side}_2}{\text{side}_2'} = \dfrac{\text{side}_3}{\text{side}_3'}$ and solve
:::

---

## Summary

:::tip What to remember
1. **Corresponding sides are opposite equal angles**
2. **Letter order matters:** $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ means A↔P, B↔Q, C↔R
3. **Always use complete sides** — not partial segments (unless the question asks about segments)
4. **In a figure:** mark angle arcs with different symbols before writing any ratio
5. **Most common figure type:** parallel line inside triangle → AA similarity
:::
