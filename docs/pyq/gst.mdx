# 📦 PYQ — GST

:::tip Strategy
GST questions always follow a chain: MP → Discount → SP → GST → Bill.
Write each step clearly — ICSE awards step marks.
:::

<PYQCard year="2024" marks="4" qnum="3(a)"
  solution={<>
    <strong>Step 1:</strong> SP = 15,000 × (100 − 20)/100 = 15,000 × 80/100 = ₹12,000<br/>
    <strong>Step 2:</strong> CGST = SGST = 9% (since GST = 18% intra-state)<br/>
    <strong>Step 3:</strong> CGST = 9/100 × 12,000 = ₹1,080; SGST = ₹1,080<br/>
    <strong>Step 4:</strong> Bill = 12,000 + 1,080 + 1,080 = <strong>₹14,160</strong>
  </>}
>
  A shopkeeper marks an article at ₹15,000 and gives a discount of 20%.
  GST is charged at 18% (intra-state). Find the CGST, SGST, and the amount paid by the customer.
</PYQCard>

<PYQCard year="2023" marks="3" qnum="4(b)"
  solution={<>
    <strong>Step 1:</strong> Let the marked price = ₹x<br/>
    <strong>Step 2:</strong> SP (after 10% discount) = x × 90/100 = 9x/10<br/>
    <strong>Step 3:</strong> Bill with 12% GST = 9x/10 × 112/100 = 28,224<br/>
    <strong>Step 4:</strong> x = 28,224 × 10/9 × 100/112 = 28,224 × 1000/1008 = <strong>₹28,000</strong>
  </>}
>
  After a 10% discount and 12% GST, a customer pays ₹28,224. Find the marked price of the article.
</PYQCard>
