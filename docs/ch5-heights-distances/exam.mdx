# 📏 Heights & Distances — Exam Cram <span className="exam-badge">🔥 EXAM READY</span>

## Key Formulas

| Situation | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| Find height | `h = d × tan θ` |
| Find distance | `d = h / tan θ = h × cot θ` |
| Find line of sight | `l = h / sin θ = d / cos θ` |
| Two angles, same object | Set up two equations, eliminate unknown |

## Values to Know by Heart

| Ratio | 30° | 45° | 60° |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|
| tan | 1/√3 | 1 | √3 |
| sin | 1/2 | 1/√2 | √3/2 |
| cos | √3/2 | 1/√2 | 1/2 |

**√3 = 1.732, 1/√3 = 0.577, √2 = 1.414**

## 5-Step Method (every problem)

1. **Draw** a clear diagram
2. **Label** angle θ, height h, distance d
3. **Write** the tan equation: `tan θ = h / d`
4. **Substitute** known values
5. **Solve** and state units

## Worked Examples

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="q1" label="Q1 — Basic">

> A pole 6 m high casts a shadow 6√3 m long. Find the angle of elevation of the sun.

tan θ = 6 / 6√3 = 1/√3 → **θ = 30°**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Angle of elevation = 30°</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q2" label="Q2 — Find Height">

> The angle of elevation of the top of a building from a point 30 m away is 60°. Find the height.

tan 60° = h / 30 → √3 = h/30 → **h = 30√3 m ≈ 51.96 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Height = 30√3 m</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q3" label="Q3 — Depression">

> From the top of a tower 75 m high, angle of depression of two cars on either side are 45° and 30°. Find distance between the cars.

- Car A (45°): tan 45° = 75/d₁ → d₁ = 75 m
- Car B (30°): tan 30° = 75/d₂ → d₂ = 75√3 m
- Distance = 75 + 75√3 = **75(1 + √3) m ≈ 204.9 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Distance = 75(1 + √3) m ≈ 204.9 m</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q4" label="Q4 — Two Angles">

> From two points A and B, 100 m apart, the angles of elevation of a tower are 45° and 60°. Find height. (A is closer.)

Let h = height, d = distance from A.

- From A: tan 45° = h/d → d = h ...(i)
- From B: tan 60° = h/(d+100) → √3(d+100) = h ...(ii)
- From (i): d = h → sub in (ii): √3(h+100) = h
- √3h + 100√3 = h → h(√3−1) = −100√3 ... rearrange:
- h(√3−1) = 100√3 → **h = 100√3/(√3−1) = 50√3(√3+1) m ≈ 236.6 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ h = 50√3(√3+1) m ≈ 236.6 m</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q5" label="Q5 — Pole + Tower">

> From top of a pole 20 m tall: elevation of tower top = 60°, depression of tower foot = 30°. Find tower height.

Let tower height = H, horizontal distance = d.

- Depression of foot → tan 30° = 20/d → d = 20√3 m
- Elevation of top → tan 60° = (H−20)/d → √3 = (H−20)/(20√3)
- H − 20 = 20√3 × √3 = 60 → **H = 80 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Tower height = 80 m</div>

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Pre-Exam Checklist

- [ ] Draw diagram for every problem — no exceptions
- [ ] tan θ = opposite / adjacent = h / d
- [ ] Know tan 30° = 1/√3, tan 45° = 1, tan 60° = √3
- [ ] Angle of depression = angle of elevation (alternate angles)
- [ ] Two-triangle problems: write TWO equations, then solve simultaneously
- [ ] If observer height given, add it to the final answer
- [ ] Maximum 2 right-angled triangles in ICSE questions
- [ ] Leave answer in surd form (like 30√3) unless decimal asked
- [ ] Line of sight: use sin (h/sin θ) or cos (d/cos θ)
- [ ] Check: does your answer make physical sense?
