## 2. Core Concepts — CPA

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="concrete" label="🧱 Concrete">

**Two real-world ideas:**

**Angle of Elevation** — You are BELOW looking UP at something.
Stand at ground level, look up at a bird on a tree. The angle your eyes make above the horizontal = angle of elevation.

**Angle of Depression** — You are ABOVE looking DOWN at something.
Stand on a cliff, look down at a boat. The angle your eyes make below the horizontal = angle of depression.

**Key fact:** Angle of elevation and angle of depression are **alternate angles** — they are equal when the horizontal lines are parallel.

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="pictorial" label="🖼️ Pictorial">

<HeightDistanceDiagram type="both" />

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="abstract" label="🔢 Abstract">

In every problem, you have a right triangle. The three sides are:

| Side | What it represents |
|------|--------------------|
| **Opposite** (height h) | Vertical distance — height of tower, cliff, etc. |
| **Adjacent** (distance d) | Horizontal distance from observer to base |
| **Hypotenuse** | Direct line of sight (rarely needed) |

<div className="fpill">tan θ = opposite / adjacent = h / d</div>
<div className="fpill fpill-teal">h = d × tan θ</div>
<div className="fpill fpill-teal">d = h / tan θ</div>

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## 3. Formulas in 4 Languages

### tan θ = h / d

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="words" label="🗣️ Words">
    The tangent of the angle of elevation (or depression) equals the height divided by the horizontal distance.
    If you know any two of these three — angle, height, distance — you can always find the third.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="visual" label="👁️ Visual">
    The diagram in Section 2 shows both cases. The triangle always has:
    - θ at the observer's position
    - h as the vertical side (opposite to θ)
    - d as the horizontal side (adjacent to θ)
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="math" label="🔣 Math">

  <div className="fpill">tan θ = h / d</div>
  <div className="fpill fpill-teal">h = d tan θ</div>
  <div className="fpill fpill-teal">d = h / tan θ = h cot θ</div>

  Also useful: `sin θ = h / (line of sight)` and `cos θ = d / (line of sight)`

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="try" label="✋ Try It">

  **A tower is 50 m tall. Observer stands 50 m away. Find angle of elevation.**

  tan θ = h / d = 50/50 = 1 → θ = **45°**

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## 4. Problem-Solving Strategy

Every ICSE Heights & Distances question follows this pattern:

1. **Draw a diagram** — never skip this (most marks lost here)
2. **Label** the known sides and the angle
3. **Identify** which ratio to use: if you have opp and adj → **tan**; if hypotenuse is involved → sin or cos
4. **Set up** the equation: `tan θ = h / d`
5. **Substitute** the known values and solve
6. **Check** units and reasonableness

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">⚠️ Always draw the diagram first</div>
  <p>Most errors come from mislabeling which side is opposite and which is adjacent.
  A clean diagram takes 30 seconds and prevents 3-mark errors.</p>
</div>

## 5. Worked Examples

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="basic" label="📗 Basic (1 Triangle)">

**Q1 — Find height**

> A boy stands 40 m from the base of a tower. The angle of elevation of the top is 30°. Find the height of the tower.

**Diagram:** Observer at ground, tower vertical, angle 30° at observer.

1. tan 30° = h / 40
2. 1/√3 = h / 40
3. h = 40/√3 = 40√3/3 = **23.1 m** (or leave as 40/√3)

<div className="ans-box">✓ Height = 40/√3 m ≈ 23.1 m</div>

**Q2 — Find distance**

> From the top of a lighthouse 60 m high, the angle of depression of a boat is 45°. Find the distance of the boat from the base of the lighthouse.

1. Angle of depression = 45° = angle of elevation from boat (alternate angles)
2. tan 45° = 60 / d → 1 = 60/d → d = **60 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Distance = 60 m</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="two" label="📙 Two Triangles">

**Q3 — Two angles, same tower**

> A man observes a tower from two points A and B on the same horizontal line. A is 100 m closer to the tower. The angles of elevation from A and B are 60° and 30° respectively. Find the height of the tower.

**Diagram:** Tower PQ, observer at A (closer), observer at B (further). Let PA = x, so PB = x + 100. Height = h.

1. From A: tan 60° = h/x → √3 = h/x → h = x√3 ... (i)
2. From B: tan 30° = h/(x+100) → 1/√3 = h/(x+100) → h = (x+100)/√3 ... (ii)
3. Equate (i) and (ii): x√3 = (x+100)/√3
4. 3x = x + 100 → 2x = 100 → x = 50 m
5. h = 50√3 = **86.6 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Height = 50√3 m ≈ 86.6 m</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="elevation-depression" label="📘 Elev + Depression">

**Q4 — From top of cliff**

> From the top of a cliff 100 m high, the angles of depression of two boats on the same side are 30° and 45°. Find the distance between the boats.

**Diagram:** Cliff PQ = 100 m. Boat A at angle 45°, Boat B at angle 30° (further away).

1. From P, boat A: tan 45° = 100/d₁ → d₁ = 100 m
2. From P, boat B: tan 30° = 100/d₂ → 1/√3 = 100/d₂ → d₂ = 100√3 m
3. Distance between boats = d₂ − d₁ = 100√3 − 100 = **100(√3 − 1) m ≈ 73.2 m**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Distance = 100(√3 − 1) m ≈ 73.2 m</div>

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
