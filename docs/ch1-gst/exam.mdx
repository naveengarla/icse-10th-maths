## All Formulas

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="standard" label="📗 Standard">

| Formula | Expression | Note |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Selling Price | `SP = MP × (100−d)/100` | d = discount % |
| GST Amount | `GST = r/100 × SP` | r = GST rate % |
| Final Bill | `Bill = SP × (100+r)/100` | one-step multiplier |
| Intra-state split | `CGST = SGST = r/2 %` | each applied to SP |
| **Master formula** | `Bill = MP × (100−d)/100 × (100+r)/100` | discount + GST combined |

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="reverse" label="📙 Reverse">

| Formula | Expression | Note |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Price before GST | `SP = A × 100/(100+r)` | A = inclusive price |
| GST inside price | `GST = A × r/(100+r)` | or: A − SP |
| Find MP from bill | `MP = Bill ÷ (100−d)/100 ÷ (100+r)/100` | divide out multipliers |
| Inter-state | `IGST = Full GST Rate` | no CGST/SGST |

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="itc" label="📘 ITC">

| Formula | Expression | Note |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Output Tax | `r/100 × SP` | GST collected from buyer |
| Input Tax | `r/100 × CP` | GST paid to supplier |
| Net GST Payable | `Output − Input Tax Credit` | |
| **Shortcut** | `(r/100) × (SP − CP)` | only if same rate on buy & sell |

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="misc" label="📕 More">

| Formula | Expression | Note |
|---------|-----------|------|
| GST rate change | `Change = Base × (r₁−r₂)/100` | Base = pre-tax price |
| Successive discounts | `d₁+d₂ − d₁d₂/100` | equivalent single discount |
| Step-by-step | `MP × (1−d₁/100) × (1−d₂/100)` | safer than the shortcut |

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## ⚠️ Top 6 Traps

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">TRAP 1 — GST on SP, not MP</div>
  <p>Wrong: <code>18% × ₹40,000 = ₹7,200</code> &nbsp; Right: <code>18% × ₹36,000 = ₹6,480</code></p>
</div>

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">TRAP 2 — Successive Discounts</div>
  <p>10% + 5% ≠ 15%. Apply one at a time: <code>MP × 0.9 × 0.95</code>.</p>
</div>

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">TRAP 3 — IGST vs CGST+SGST</div>
  <p>Inter-state → IGST only. Intra-state → CGST + SGST. Never both on the same transaction.</p>
</div>

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">TRAP 4 — Reverse GST</div>
  <p>Price includes 12% GST → <code>SP = Price × 100/112</code>.<br />
  Wrong: <code>SP = Price − 12% of Price</code> (circular error).</p>
</div>

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">TRAP 5 — ITC Confusion</div>
  <p>Net GST = Output Tax − Input Tax Credit. Consumer gets NO ITC.</p>
</div>

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">TRAP 6 — Profit Excludes GST</div>
  <p>Profit = SP (pre-GST) − CP (pre-GST). GST is never the seller's profit.</p>
</div>

## Worked Examples

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="q1" label="Q1 — Basic">

> **TV: MP ₹45,000, 15% discount, 28% GST (intra-state). Find SP, CGST, SGST, Bill.**

1. SP = 45,000 × 85/100 = **₹38,250**
2. CGST = SGST = 14% → 14/100 × 38,250 = **₹5,355 each**
3. Bill = 38,250 + 5,355 + 5,355 = **₹48,960**

<div className="ans-box">SP = ₹38,250 | CGST = SGST = ₹5,355 | Bill = ₹48,960</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q2" label="Q2 — Reverse">

> **Customer pays ₹5,900 including 18% GST. Find original price and GST.**

1. SP = 5,900 × 100/118 = **₹5,000**
2. GST = 5,900 − 5,000 = **₹900**

<div className="ans-box">Original Price = ₹5,000 | GST = ₹900</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q3" label="Q3 — ITC">

> **Wholesaler: buys ₹40,000, sells ₹55,000. GST 12%. Net GST + invoice.**

1. Input Tax = 12% × 40,000 = ₹4,800
2. Output Tax = 12% × 55,000 = ₹6,600
3. Net GST = 6,600 − 4,800 = **₹1,800**
4. Invoice = 55,000 + 6,600 = **₹61,600**

<div className="ans-box">Net GST = ₹1,800 | Invoice = ₹61,600</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q4" label="Q4 — Find MP">

> **Customer pays ₹28,320 after 20% discount + 18% GST. Find MP.**

1. Let MP = x → SP = x × 80/100
2. Bill = SP × 118/100 = 28,320
3. x × 80/100 × 118/100 = 28,320
4. x = 28,320 × 10000/9440 = **₹30,000**

<div className="ans-box">Marked Price = ₹30,000</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q5" label="Q5 — Successive">

> **MP ₹10,000, discounts 10% and 5%, GST 12%. Bill?**

1. After 1st: 10,000 × 90/100 = ₹9,000
2. After 2nd: 9,000 × 95/100 = ₹8,550
3. Bill: 8,550 × 112/100 = **₹9,576**

<div className="ans-box">Bill = ₹9,576</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="q6" label="Q6 — Rate Change">

> **Pre-tax price ₹30,000. GST drops 28% → 18%. How much cheaper?**

Shortcut: 30,000 × (28−18)/100 = **₹3,000**

<div className="ans-box">Price reduced by ₹3,000</div>

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Pre-Exam Checklist

- [ ] CGST = SGST = GST rate ÷ 2 (intra-state only)
- [ ] GST always on SP (after discount), never on MP
- [ ] Successive discounts: apply one by one, never add
- [ ] Reverse: `SP = Inclusive × 100/(100+r)`
- [ ] Net GST = Output Tax − Input Tax Credit
- [ ] Inter-state = IGST only; never with CGST/SGST
- [ ] Consumer bears full GST, no ITC for them
- [ ] Profit calculated on pre-GST prices
- [ ] GST rate change: `Change = Base × Δr/100`
- [ ] Multi-item bill: GST per item, then grand total
