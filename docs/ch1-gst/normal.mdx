# 📦 GST — Deep Learning

## 1. The Story (Finland Phenomenon)

<div className="story-card">
  <div className="story-label">🎮 Arjun's Gaming Rig</div>
  <p>
    <strong>Arjun</strong> walks into a Delhi electronics shop to buy a graphics card.
    The sticker says <strong>₹40,000</strong> (Marked Price).
    The shopkeeper offers a <strong>10% festival discount</strong>.
    The government charges <strong>18% GST</strong> on electronics.
    Both seller and buyer are in Delhi — <strong>intra-state</strong> transaction.
    <br /><br />
    How much does Arjun actually pay? Where does the tax money go?
    Why is the tax <em>not</em> on ₹40,000?
    Every formula in this chapter answers one of those three questions.
  </p>
</div>

Before GST (pre-2017), India had VAT, Excise Duty, Service Tax, Entry Tax — each charged differently.
A single product could be taxed 5–6 times from factory to consumer.
**GST replaced all of these** with one unified tax.

| Type | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| **Direct Tax** | Paid directly by the earner — e.g. Income Tax |
| **Indirect Tax (GST)** | Collected from the consumer through the seller. The seller is the government's tax collector. |

---

## 2. Core Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning | Arjun's GPU |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Marked Price (M.P.)** | Price tagged before discount | ₹40,000 |
| **Discount** | `d% × M.P. ÷ 100` | ₹4,000 (10%) |
| **Selling Price (S.P.)** | After discount, before GST | ₹36,000 |
| **Taxable Value** | = S.P. — GST is applied **here** | ₹36,000 ⚠️ |
| **GST Amount** | `GST% × Taxable Value ÷ 100` | ₹6,480 |
| **CGST / SGST** | Intra-state: total GST split equally | ₹3,240 each |
| **Final Bill** | Taxable Value + GST | ₹42,480 |

## 3. Bar Model — Singapore CPA Approach

Singapore Math never introduces a formula before a student sees it concretely and pictorially first.

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="concrete" label="🧱 Concrete">

**Stage 1 — Concrete:** What physically happens to the money.

Arjun hands over ₹42,480.
The shopkeeper keeps ₹36,000 (his selling price).
He sends ₹3,240 (CGST) to the Central Government and ₹3,240 (SGST) to the Delhi State Government.
The ₹4,000 discount was a gift from the shopkeeper — the government never touches it.

| Who gets it | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| 🏪 Shopkeeper | ₹36,000 |
| 🏛️ Central Govt (CGST) | ₹3,240 |
| 🏛️ State Govt (SGST) | ₹3,240 |
| 🎁 Discount (vanishes) | ₹4,000 — no GST on this |

## 5. Formulas in 4 Languages (Finland Languaging)

Finland's research: a student owns a formula only when they can express it in 4 ways.

### Formula 1 — Standard Bill

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="words" label="🗣️ Words">
    Subtract the discount from the Marked Price to get the Selling Price (Taxable Value).
    Then add a percentage of only that amount as GST. The customer pays SP + GST.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="visual" label="👁️ Visual">

  `[MP]` → chop off discount → `[SP]` → glue on GST → `[BILL]`

  *(See the bar model in Section 3)*

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="math" label="🔣 Math">

  <div className="fpill">SP = MP × (100−d)/100</div>
  <div className="fpill">Bill = SP × (100+r)/100</div>
  <div className="fpill fpill-teal">Bill = MP × (100−d)/100 × (100+r)/100</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="try" label="✋ Try It">

  **MP = ₹5,000, Discount = 20%, GST = 12%. Find the bill.**

  - SP = 5,000 × 80/100 = **₹4,000**
  - Bill = 4,000 × 112/100 = **₹4,480**

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Formula 2 — Reverse (GST-Inclusive Price)

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="words" label="🗣️ Words">
    When the given price already includes GST, it equals (100 + r)% of the original price.
    To strip the GST out, divide by (100 + r) and multiply by 100.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="visual" label="👁️ Visual">

  `[Price A = 118% of SP]` → divide by 118 → multiply by 100 → `[SP]`

  Think: ₹118 includes ₹18 GST. So ₹100 out of every ₹118 is the actual price.

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="math" label="🔣 Math">

  <div className="fpill">SP = A × 100 / (100 + r)</div>
  <div className="fpill fpill-teal">GST in A = A × r / (100 + r)</div>

  where A = the given inclusive price, r = GST rate %

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="try" label="✋ Try It">

  **Customer pays ₹2,360 including 18% GST. Find the original price and GST.**

  - SP = 2,360 × 100/118 = **₹2,000**
  - GST = 2,360 − 2,000 = **₹360**

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Formula 3 — Net GST Payable (for Dealers)

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="words" label="🗣️ Words">
    A dealer collects GST from customers (output tax) but also paid GST when buying stock (input tax).
    The government only wants the difference — the tax on the dealer's value added.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="visual" label="👁️ Visual">

  `OUTPUT TAX` (collected from buyer)
  `−` `INPUT TAX CREDIT` (paid to supplier)
  `=` `NET GST PAYABLE`

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="math" label="🔣 Math">

  <div className="fpill">Net GST = Output Tax − Input Tax Credit</div>
  <div className="fpill fpill-teal">Net GST = (r/100) × (SP − CP)  [same rate only]</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="try" label="✋ Try It">

  **Dealer buys at ₹20,000, sells at ₹28,000. GST = 12%. Find net GST.**

  - Output Tax = 12% × 28,000 = ₹3,360
  - Input Tax  = 12% × 20,000 = ₹2,400
  - Net GST    = 3,360 − 2,400 = **₹960**
  - *Shortcut: 12% × (28,000−20,000) = 12% × 8,000 = ₹960 ✓*

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## 7. Worked Examples

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="basic" label="📗 Basic">

**Q1 — Discount + CGST + SGST**

> A television is marked at ₹45,000. A 15% discount is given. GST = 28% (intra-state).
> Find (i) S.P. (ii) CGST (iii) SGST (iv) Amount paid by buyer.

1. SP = 45,000 × 85/100 = **₹38,250**
2. CGST Rate = SGST Rate = 28/2 = **14% each**
3. CGST = SGST = 14/100 × 38,250 = **₹5,355 each**
4. Bill = 38,250 + 5,355 + 5,355 = **₹48,960**

<div className="ans-box">✓ SP = ₹38,250 | CGST = ₹5,355 | SGST = ₹5,355 | Bill = ₹48,960</div>

**Q2 — Successive Discounts + GST**

> MP = ₹10,000. Discounts of 10% then 5%. GST = 12%. Find bill.

1. After 1st discount: 10,000 × 90/100 = ₹9,000
2. After 2nd discount: 9,000 × 95/100 = ₹8,550 ← taxable value
3. Bill = 8,550 × 112/100 = **₹9,576**

<div className="trap-box">
  <div className="trap-title">⚠️ Why not 15%?</div>
  <p>10% + 5% = 14.5% equivalent, not 15%. Apply one by one.</p>
</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="reverse" label="📙 Reverse">

**Q3 — Price Inclusive of GST**

> Priya pays ₹7,080 for a saree that includes 18% GST. Find original price and GST.

1. ₹7,080 = 118% of SP → SP = 7,080 × 100/118 = **₹6,000**
2. GST = 7,080 − 6,000 = **₹1,080**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Price before GST = ₹6,000 | GST = ₹1,080</div>

**Q4 — Find Marked Price from Bill**

> Customer pays ₹28,320 after 20% discount and 18% GST. Find Marked Price.

1. Let MP = x. SP = x × 80/100
2. Bill = SP × 118/100 → x × 80/100 × 118/100 = 28,320
3. x = 28,320 × 10000/9440 = **₹30,000**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Marked Price = ₹30,000</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="itc" label="📘 ITC Chain">

**Q5 — Net GST Payable**

> Wholesaler buys at ₹50,000, sells at ₹65,000. GST = 12% (intra-state).
> Find: (i) Input Tax (ii) Output Tax (iii) Net GST (iv) Invoice to retailer.

1. Input Tax = 12/100 × 50,000 = **₹6,000**
2. Output Tax = 12/100 × 65,000 = **₹7,800**
3. Net GST = 7,800 − 6,000 = **₹1,800** *(Shortcut: 12% × 15,000 = ₹1,800 ✓)*
4. Invoice = 65,000 + 7,800 = **₹72,800**

<div className="ans-box">✓ Input Tax = ₹6,000 | Output Tax = ₹7,800 | Net GST = ₹1,800 | Invoice = ₹72,800</div>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem value="rate" label="📕 Rate Change">

**Q6 — GST Rate Reduction**

> Pre-tax price of an AC is ₹30,000. GST drops from 28% to 18%. By how much does the consumer's price change?

1. Old Bill = 30,000 × 128/100 = ₹38,400
2. New Bill = 30,000 × 118/100 = ₹35,400
3. Reduction = 38,400 − 35,400 = **₹3,000**
4. *Shortcut: 30,000 × (28−18)/100 = ₹3,000 ✓*

<div className="ans-box">✓ Consumer price reduced by ₹3,000</div>

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
