📐 PYQ — Trigonometric Identities (2000–2025)

Strategy

Prove: Work ONE side only. Stuck? Convert everything to sin⁡A\sin AsinA and cos⁡A\cos AcosA.

Evaluate: Replace complementary pairs first — cos⁡70°=sin⁡20°\cos 70° = \sin 20°cos70°=sin20°, tan⁡72°=cot⁡18°\tan 72° = \cot 18°tan72°=cot18°.

Year 2000​

Q1 · ICSE 2000[4 marks]

Prove: 1−cos⁡A1+cos⁡A=sin⁡A1+cos⁡A\sqrt{\dfrac{1-\cos A}{1+\cos A}} = \dfrac{\sin A}{1+\cos A}1+cosA1−cosA​​=1+cosAsinA​

Show Solution

Multiply inside x\sqrt{\phantom{x}}x​ by 1−cos⁡A1−cos⁡A\dfrac{1-\cos A}{1-\cos A}1−cosA1−cosA​:

LHS=(1−cos⁡A)2sin⁡2A=1−cos⁡Asin⁡A\text{LHS} = \sqrt{\dfrac{(1-\cos A)^2}{\sin^2 A}} = \dfrac{1-\cos A}{\sin A}LHS=sin2A(1−cosA)2​​=sinA1−cosA​

RHS: sin⁡A1+cos⁡A=sin⁡A(1−cos⁡A)sin⁡2A=1−cos⁡Asin⁡A✓\dfrac{\sin A}{1+\cos A} = \dfrac{\sin A(1-\cos A)}{\sin^2 A} = \dfrac{1-\cos A}{\sin A} \checkmark1+cosAsinA​=sin2AsinA(1−cosA)​=sinA1−cosA​✓

Q2 · ICSE 2000[4 marks]

Prove: 11+cos⁡(90°−A)+11−cos⁡(90°−A)=2cosec⁡2(90°−A)\dfrac{1}{1+\cos(90°-A)} + \dfrac{1}{1-\cos(90°-A)} = 2\operatorname{cosec}^2(90°-A)1+cos(90°−A)1​+1−cos(90°−A)1​=2cosec2(90°−A)

Show Solution

Use cos⁡(90°−A)=sin⁡A\cos(90°-A)=\sin Acos(90°−A)=sinA and cosec⁡(90°−A)=sec⁡A\operatorname{cosec}(90°-A)=\sec Acosec(90°−A)=secA:

LHS=11+sin⁡A+11−sin⁡A=21−sin⁡2A=2cos⁡2A=2sec⁡2A=2cosec⁡2(90°−A)✓\text{LHS} = \dfrac{1}{1+\sin A}+\dfrac{1}{1-\sin A} = \dfrac{2}{1-\sin^2 A} = \dfrac{2}{\cos^2 A} = 2\sec^2 A = 2\operatorname{cosec}^2(90°-A) \checkmarkLHS=1+sinA1​+1−sinA1​=1−sin2A2​=cos2A2​=2sec2A=2cosec2(90°−A)✓
