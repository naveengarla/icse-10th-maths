📐 PYQ — Trigonometric Identities (2000–2025)

Before you start

Two question types only:

  1. Prove — work ONE side only. Never cross the = sign.
  2. Evaluate — spot complementary pairs first (cos⁡70°=sin⁡20°\cos 70° = \sin 20°cos70°=sin20°), then use standard values.

Year 2000​

Q1 · ICSE 2000[4 marks]

Prove: 1−cos⁡A1+cos⁡A=sin⁡A1+cos⁡A\sqrt{\dfrac{1-\cos A}{1+\cos A}} = \dfrac{\sin A}{1+\cos A}1+cosA1−cosA​​=1+cosAsinA​

Show Solution

🔍 TYPE: Square root fraction — rationalize by multiplying inside by the numerator.

💡 TRICK: When you see ab\sqrt{\dfrac{a}{b}}ba​​, multiply top and bottom inside by aaa to make a perfect square in the numerator. Then the \sqrt{}​ vanishes.

Step 1 — Rationalize LHS: Multiply inside \sqrt{}​ by 1−cos⁡A1−cos⁡A\dfrac{1-\cos A}{1-\cos A}1−cosA1−cosA​:

LHS=(1−cos⁡A)2(1+cos⁡A)(1−cos⁡A)=(1−cos⁡A)21−cos⁡2A\text{LHS} = \sqrt{\dfrac{(1-\cos A)^2}{(1+\cos A)(1-\cos A)}} = \sqrt{\dfrac{(1-\cos A)^2}{1-\cos^2 A}}LHS=(1+cosA)(1−cosA)(1−cosA)2​​=1−cos2A(1−cosA)2​​

Step 2 — Use identity 1: 1−cos⁡2A=sin⁡2A1-\cos^2 A = \sin^2 A1−cos2A=sin2A:

=(1−cos⁡A)2sin⁡2A=1−cos⁡Asin⁡A= \sqrt{\dfrac{(1-\cos A)^2}{\sin^2 A}} = \dfrac{1-\cos A}{\sin A}=sin2A(1−cosA)2​​=sinA1−cosA​

Step 3 — Show RHS equals same: Multiply RHS top and bottom by (1−cos⁡A)(1-\cos A)(1−cosA):

sin⁡A1+cos⁡A=sin⁡A(1−cos⁡A)1−cos⁡2A=sin⁡A(1−cos⁡A)sin⁡2A=1−cos⁡Asin⁡A✓\dfrac{\sin A}{1+\cos A} = \dfrac{\sin A(1-\cos A)}{1-\cos^2 A} = \dfrac{\sin A(1-\cos A)}{\sin^2 A} = \dfrac{1-\cos A}{\sin A} \checkmark1+cosAsinA​=1−cos2AsinA(1−cosA)​=sin2AsinA(1−cosA)​=sinA1−cosA​✓

⚠️ Common mistake: Trying to simplify 1−cos⁡A1+cos⁡A\sqrt{\dfrac{1-\cos A}{1+\cos A}}1+cosA1−cosA​​ directly. You can't — always rationalize first.

Q2 · ICSE 2000[4 marks]

Prove: 11+cos⁡(90°−A)+11−cos⁡(90°−A)=2cosec⁡2(90°−A)\dfrac{1}{1+\cos(90°-A)}+\dfrac{1}{1-\cos(90°-A)} = 2\operatorname{cosec}^2(90°-A)1+cos(90°−A)1​+1−cos(90°−A)1​=2cosec2(90°−A)

Show Solution

🔍 TYPE: Complementary angle substitution.

💡 TRICK: Spot the 90°−A90°-A90°−A angles immediately. Replace them using cos⁡(90°−A)=sin⁡A\cos(90°-A)=\sin Acos(90°−A)=sinA and cosec⁡(90°−A)=sec⁡A\operatorname{cosec}(90°-A)=\sec Acosec(90°−A)=secA. After that it's just adding two fractions.

Step 1 — Replace: cos⁡(90°−A)=sin⁡A\cos(90°-A)=\sin Acos(90°−A)=sinA, cosec⁡(90°−A)=sec⁡A\operatorname{cosec}(90°-A)=\sec Acosec(90°−A)=secA:

LHS=11+sin⁡A+11−sin⁡A\text{LHS} = \dfrac{1}{1+\sin A}+\dfrac{1}{1-\sin A}LHS=1+sinA1​+1−sinA1​

Step 2 — Add fractions (common denominator = (1+sin⁡A)(1−sin⁡A)=1−sin⁡2A(1+\sin A)(1-\sin A) = 1-\sin^2 A(1+sinA)(1−sinA)=1−sin2A):

=(1−sin⁡A)+(1+sin⁡A)1−sin⁡2A=21−sin⁡2A= \dfrac{(1-\sin A)+(1+\sin A)}{1-\sin^2 A} = \dfrac{2}{1-\sin^2 A}=1−sin2A(1−sinA)+(1+sinA)​=1−sin2A2​

Step 3 — Use identity: 1−sin⁡2A=cos⁡2A1-\sin^2 A = \cos^2 A1−sin2A=cos2A:

=2cos⁡2A=2sec⁡2A=2cosec⁡2(90°−A)=RHS✓= \dfrac{2}{\cos^2 A} = 2\sec^2 A = 2\operatorname{cosec}^2(90°-A) = \text{RHS} \checkmark=cos2A2​=2sec2A=2cosec2(90°−A)=RHS✓

✅ Exam tip: Always do the 90°−A90°-A90°−A replacement as your very first step. Never leave it for later.

Show Solution

🔍 TYPE: Find AAA first, then verify both sides numerically.

💡 TRICK: This is NOT a proof — it's a verification. Find AAA from the given equation, then check LHS = RHS by substituting.

✅ Exam tip: Write "LHS = 1" and "RHS = 1" clearly. Then state "LHS = RHS, hence proved."

Q1 · ICSE 2003[4 marks]

Prove: cos⁡A1−tan⁡A+sin⁡A1−cot⁡A=sin⁡A+cos⁡A\dfrac{\cos A}{1-\tan A}+\dfrac{\sin A}{1-\cot A} = \sin A+\cos A1−tanAcosA​+1−cotAsinA​=sinA+cosA

✅ Exam tip: The factor (cosA+sinA)(cosA−sinA)(cosA+sinA)(cosA-sinA)(cosA+sinA)(cosA−sinA) cancelling the denominator is the key step. Show it.
