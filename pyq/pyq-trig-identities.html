📐 PYQ — Trigonometry Identities (2000–2025)

Strategy

For Prove questions: Work on ONE side only. If stuck — convert everything to sin and cos. For Evaluate questions: Replace complementary angles (cos70° = sin20°, etc.) first, then simplify.

Year 2000​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2000[4 marks]

Prove: √[(1−cosA)/(1+cosA)] = sinA/(1+cosA)

Q2  ·  ICSE 2000[4 marks]

Prove: 1/(1+cos(90°−A)) + 1/(1−cos(90°−A)) = 2cosec²(90°−A)

Year 2001​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2001[4 marks]

Prove: √[(1−sinA)/(1+sinA)] = cosA/(1+sinA)

Q2  ·  ICSE 2001[4 marks]

If 2sinA − 1 = 0, show that sin3A = 3sinA − 4sin³A.

Year 2002​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2002[4 marks]

Prove: 1/(sinA+cosA) + 1/(sinA−cosA) = 2sinA/(1−2cos²A)

Q2  ·  ICSE 2002[4 marks]

Prove: 1/(1+sin(90°−A)) + 1/(1−sin(90°−A)) = 2sec²(90°−A)

Year 2003​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2003[4 marks]

Prove: cosA/(1−tanA) + sinA/(1−cotA) = sinA + cosA

Year 2004​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2004[4 marks]

Prove: (cotA + tanB)/(cotB + tanA) = cotA tanB

Q2  ·  ICSE 2004[3 marks]

Evaluate: 14sin30° + 6cos60° − 5tan45°

Year 2005​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2005[4 marks]

Prove: (1−tanA)² + (1+tanA)² = 2sec²A

Year 2006​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2006[4 marks]

Prove: (1+tan²A) + (1 + 1/tan²A) = 1/(sin²A − sin⁴A)

Year 2007​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2007[4 marks]

Prove: 1/(secA + tanA) = secA − tanA

Q2  ·  ICSE 2007[3 marks]

Evaluate: cos70°/sin20° + cos59°/sin31° − 8sin²30°

Year 2008​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2008[4 marks]

Prove: (sinA tanA)/(1−cosA) = 1 + secA

Year 2009​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2009[4 marks]

Prove: (cotA + tanB)/(cotB + tanA) = cotA tanB

Year 2010​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2010[3 marks]

Evaluate: sin35°cos55° + cos35°sin55°

Q2  ·  ICSE 2010[3 marks]

Evaluate: cosec²10° − tan²80°

Year 2011​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2011[3 marks]

Evaluate: cos75°/sin15° + sin12°/cos78° − cos18°/sin72°

Q2  ·  ICSE 2011[4 marks]

Prove: (cosecA − sinA)(secA − cosA)sec²A = tanA

Year 2012​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2012[4 marks]

Prove: (1+cosA)/(1−cosA) = tan²A/(secA−1)²

Year 2013​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2013[4 marks]

Prove: √[(1−cosA)/(1+cosA)] = sinA/(1+cosA)

Q2  ·  ICSE 2013[3 marks]

Evaluate: cot²41°/tan²49° − 2sin²75°/cos²15°

Year 2014​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2014[3 marks]

Evaluate: sin²34° + sin²56° + 2tan18°tan72° − cot²30°

Q2  ·  ICSE 2014[4 marks]

Prove: (sinθ+cosθ)(tanθ+cotθ) = secθ + cosecθ

Year 2015​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2015[4 marks]

Prove: cosA/(1−tanA) + sinA/(1−cotA) = cosA + sinA

Year 2016​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2016[3 marks]

Evaluate: csc²57° − tan²33° + cos44°·csc46° − √2·cos45° − tan²60°

Q2  ·  ICSE 2016[4 marks]

Prove: cosA/(1+sinA) + tanA = secA

Year 2017​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2017[4 marks]

Prove: (sinθ − 2sin³θ) / (2cos³θ − cosθ) = tanθ

Year 2018​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2018[4 marks]

Prove: √(sec²θ + csc²θ) = tanθ + cotθ

Q2  ·  ICSE 2018[4 marks]

Prove: (1 + cotθ − cscθ)(1 + tanθ + secθ) = 2

Year 2019​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2019[4 marks]

Prove: (cscθ − sinθ)(secθ − cosθ)(tanθ + cotθ) = 1

Year 2020​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2020[4 marks]

Prove: [(1−tanθ)/(1−cotθ)]² = tan²θ

Year 2023​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2023[4 marks]

Prove: (sin²θ − 1)(tan²θ + 1) + 1 = 0

Q2 (MCQ)  ·  ICSE 2023[1 marks]

The value of (1+sinA)(1−sinA) is equal to ___

Year 2024​

Q1  ·  ICSE 2024[4 marks]

Prove: (sin³θ + cos³θ)/(sinθ + cosθ) + sinθcosθ = 1
