📏 PYQ — Heights & Distances (2000–2025)

About these questions

The original board paper text for Heights & Distances was not available in full. All questions below are original practice questions created at the same difficulty level, same question types, and same mark weightings as the actual ICSE board papers for each year. They are labelled Practice instead of year to be transparent.

Strategy — every question, every time
  1. Draw the diagram — label θ, h, d before writing anything
  2. Identify which trig ratio connects what you know to what you need
  3. Set up the equation, substitute, solve
  4. Check units and whether the answer is physically reasonable

Window & Tower (2000-style)​

Q2000-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

A window in a building is 8 m above the ground. From the window, the angle of elevation of the top of a tower on the opposite side of the road is 60° and the angle of depression of the base of the tower is 30°. Find (i) the height of the tower and (ii) the width of the road.

Tower with Given Trig Value (2001 Q1-style)​

Q2001a-type · ICSE Practice[3 marks]

A man stands 100 m from the base of a vertical tower. He observes the angle of elevation of the top to be θ, where cosθ = 0.6. Find (i) the height of the tower and (ii) the distance from the man to the top of the tower.

Walking Towards a Building (2001 Q2-style)​

Q2001b-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

A man observes the angle of elevation of the top of a building to be 30°. He walks 60 m straight towards the building and finds the angle of elevation is now 60°. Find the height of the building.

River and Tree (2003-style)​

Q2003-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

A tree stands vertically on the bank of a river. From a point P directly opposite on the other bank, the angle of elevation of the top of the tree is 60°. From a point Q, 20 m further along the same bank from P, the angle of elevation is 30°. Find the height of the tree and the width of the river.

Two Observers and Tower (2004-style)​

Q2004-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

From the top of a vertical tower 70 m high, the angles of depression of two points A and B on the same horizontal ground and on the same side of the tower are 60° and 30° respectively (A is closer to the tower). Find the distance between A and B.

Shadow Problem (2006-style)​

Q2006-type · ICSE Practice[3 marks]

The shadow of a vertical pole is 3 m longer when the angle of elevation of the sun is 30° than when it is 60°. Find the height of the pole.

Hill and Distance Points (2007-style)​

Q2007-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

A person on the bank of a river observes that the angle of elevation of a tree on the opposite bank is 60°. When he moves 40 m back from the bank, the angle of elevation becomes 30°. Find the height of the tree and the width of the river.

Pole and Tower (2008-style)​

Q2008-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

A vertical pole 6 m tall and a vertical tower stand on the same horizontal ground. From the top of the pole, the angle of elevation of the top of the tower is 60° and the angle of depression of the foot of the tower is 30°. Find the height of the tower and the horizontal distance between them.

Lighthouse and Two Ships (2010-style)​

Q2010-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

From the top of a lighthouse 75 m high, the angles of depression of two ships on the same side of the lighthouse are 45° and 30°. Find the distance between the two ships.

Building and Lamp Post (2012-style)​

Q2012-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

From the top of a building 20 m high, the angle of depression of the foot of a lamp post is 30° and the angle of elevation of its top is 45°. Find the height of the lamp post.

Aeroplane Over River (2014-style)​

Q2014-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

An aeroplane flies horizontally and observes two points on opposite banks of a river directly below. The angles of depression of the two points are 45° and 30°. If the width of the river is 100 m, find the height at which the aeroplane is flying.

Two Towers (2015-style)​

Q2015-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

Two vertical towers stand 120 m apart on horizontal ground. The angle of elevation of the top of the first tower from the foot of the second is 30° and the angle of elevation of the top of the second tower from the foot of the first is 60°. Find the heights of the two towers.

Aeroplane and Two Ships (2016-style)​

Q2016-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

An aeroplane at a height of 3000 m observes two ships sailing in the same direction. The angles of depression of the ships are 45° and 30°. Find the distance between the two ships.

Two Towers — Elevation and Depression (2018-style)​

Q2018-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

Tower A is 25 m tall. From the top of tower A, the angle of elevation of the top of tower B is 45° and the angle of depression of the foot of tower B is 30°. Find the height of tower B and the horizontal distance between the two towers.

Cliff and Tower (2020-style)​

Q2020-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

From the top of a cliff 150 m high, the angle of depression of the base of a tower is 30° and the angle of elevation of the top of the tower is 45°. Find the height of the tower.

Tower and Ships on Opposite Sides (2023-style)​

Q2023-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

From the top of a tower 100 m high, the angles of depression of two ships on opposite sides of the tower are 60° and 45°. Find the distance between the two ships.

Tree Observed from Opposite Sides (2024-style)​

Q2024-type · ICSE Practice[3 marks]

A tree 100 m tall stands vertically on level ground. From two points A and B on opposite sides of the tree, the angles of elevation of the top are 45° and 30° respectively. Find the distance between A and B.

Building and Tower — Elevation & Depression (2025 Q1-style)​

Q2025a-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

A building 20 m high stands on one side of a road. A tower stands on the other side. From the top of the building, the angle of elevation of the top of the tower is 60° and the angle of depression of the foot of the tower is 30°. Find the height of the tower.

Hill and Tower (2025 Q2-style)​

Q2025b-type · ICSE Practice[4 marks]

The angle of elevation of the top of a hill from the foot of a tower is 60° and the angle of elevation of the top of the tower from the foot of the hill is 45°. If the tower is 50 m tall, find the height of the hill and the horizontal distance between them.
